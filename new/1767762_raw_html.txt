           <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:justify;">
            <font color="#2A2A2A">
             When you're just starting out as an independent adult, getting a job and seeking the direction of your life, it's easy to feel afraid of failure. Maybe it feels like everyone around you is succeeding, jumping from one victory to another. Maybe you decide not to change direction of your life and start doing what you believe in and have a passion for - all because of your fear of failure. This fear is inherent for us humans. We have a vain idea that everyone around us will rejoice in our failure.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             There is one thing we must understand. Success does not come without failure or the risk of failure.
             <span style="background-color: initial;">
              One of the biggest misunderstandings of our time is that success is a product of 'luck'. We believe that success is a swift process, that it comes overnight to those who have the financial means and connections for it.
             </span>
            </font>
            <br/>
            <font color="#2A2A2A">
             <span style="background-color: initial;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </font>
            <br/>
            <font color="#2A2A2A">
             <span style="background-color: initial;">
              As Dustin Moskowitz, co-founder of Facebook, put it: when Moskowitz was asked about how it was to be part of Facebook's "overnight success", he answered:
             </span>
            </font>
            <font color="#2A2A2A">
             If by overnight success you mean staying up and coding all night, every night for six years straight, then it felt really tiring and stressful.
            </font>
            <font color="#2A2A2A">
             <span style="background-color: initial;">
              <br/>
             </span>
             <br/>
             We've compiled a list of 29 extremely successful people throughout history, who all went through failures and hard times in their lifetime.
            </font>

             1. Vincent Van Gogh
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>
             During his lifetime, Vincent Van Gogh only sold one out of 900 of his paintings. Today, he is one of the most recognized artists. Many of his paintings have been auctioned at record sums.

            <span>
            </span>

             2. Abraham Lincoln
             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>
             Lincoln started out as a war captain and got degraded to private (essentially, he got degraded to the lowest of ranks). He started several businesses that failed and was defeated in numerous runs, before he became one of the greatest leaders throughout the history of our nation.
            </font>

             3. Emily Dickinson
             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span>

             4. Orville and Wilbur Wright
             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>
             The Wright brothers battled with depression and family illness, before starting a bicycle shop that led them on to experimenting with flight. They tried and failed numerous times, and after several years of work and failed prototypes, they got their plane to stay in air.

           <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:justify;">
            <font color="#2A2A2A">
             5. Thomas Edison
             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>
             Thomas Edison was told by teachers that he was too stupid to learn anything.
            </font>
            <font color="#2A2A2A">
             He had to try 1,000 times before he successfully created the light bulb. In the end,

             <span>
             </span>

He launched most of the companys most popular products, like the iPod, iPhone and iPad. He also co-founded Pixar and made it a leading animation studio.

Later, Winfrey was fired from her first television job. Today, Winfrey is the most influential TV talk show hostess, has built a media empire and is worth around $2.9 billion.

             <span>

             <br/>
             <span>
             </span>

Her mother was diagnosed with MS, she divorced her husband, who by some accounts was violent, and during the time that she wrote the first Harry Potter novel, she was a single, unemployed mum. She was on welfare and was as poor as you can get in Britain without being homeless at one point. "Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone" got rejected 12 times. For nine years  after her success with Harry Potter  she was estranged from her father. Today, she is one of the best known and best paid authors of all time.
